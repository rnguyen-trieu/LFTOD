The Problem


- According to the open data available on the site created for the Cajun Code Fest competition, a social issue facing our community of Lafayette is the road conditions.

- In 2016, 34% of all car accidents reported in Lafayette occurred on just ten of the many roads that were reported to have either poor or very poor road conditions.

- According to the Daily Advertiser, 60% of major local and state roads in the Lafayette area are in poor or mediocre condition.

- A study released earlier this year shows Lafayette drivers pay an average of $2,024 per year because of roads that are deteriorated, congested or lack safety features.

- The correlation between poor road conditions and the number of car accidents needs to be highlighted and discussed amongst both concerned citizens and Lafayette city government officials.
